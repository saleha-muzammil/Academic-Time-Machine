Instructions: Argumentative Essay

Dear Students,
Please read the following instructions carefully to understand the structure and expectations for your argumentative essay:

1. Understanding the Thesis Statement
In argumentative essays, the thesis statement has a specific structure. It usually includes:
• Counter-argument (with topic embedded)
• Your claim (your position)
• Supporting reasons (2–3 arguments)

2. Important Concept: Topic Placement
Unlike other essays, you do NOT mention the topic separately at the beginning.
Instead:
The topic is embedded within the counter-argument.

3. Key Example (Must Understand)
“Some people believe that classmates play a greater role in shaping a child’s academic success (Counter-Argument with topic embedded); however (Refutation), parents remain the most important influence (Claim) because they provide early learning foundations (Reason 1) and consistent guidance (Reason 2).”
Here:
• Topic is embedded inside the counter-argument
• First comes counter-argument
• Then your claim
• Then supporting reasons

4. Order of Thesis Statement (VERY IMPORTANT)
You must follow this order:
1. Counter-argument (Topic Embedded)
2. Claim
3. Supporting Arguments (2 reasons)

5. Counter-Argument Paragraph Placement
• Ideally, the counter-argument paragraph should be the first body paragraph
• This helps create a smooth and logical flow
However:
You may place it later (e.g., third body paragraph),
but you must ensure a clear transition to the conclusion

6. Refutation (Important Tip)
• Refutation can be:
  • A phrase OR
  • A complete sentence
• It should appear at the end of the counter-argument paragraph
• It should act as a bridge to the next paragraph

7. General Instructions
• Follow the correct thesis structure strictly
• Ensure clarity and logical flow
• Use proper transitions between paragraphs

8. Practice Tasks
As discussed in class:
• Practice writing thesis statements using the correct structure
• Identify for the topics shared:
  • Counter-argument
  • Claim
  • Supporting reasons
• Practice writing counter-argument paragraphs with refutation
