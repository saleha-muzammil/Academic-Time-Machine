# 1. What is Neo-Liberalism?

Neo-liberalism is a modern political-economic ideology that promotes:

- Free markets
- Privatization
- Deregulation
- Limited government intervention
- Competition
- Individual responsibility

---

# Simple Definition

> Neo-liberalism believes society works best when markets are free and the government interferes as little as possible.

---

# Historical Context

Neo-liberalism became popular after:

- economic crises of the 1970s

It became dominant in:

- USA
- UK
- IMF/World Bank policies

Major leaders:

- Ronald Reagan (USA)
- Margaret Thatcher (UK)

---

# 2. Neo-Liberalism vs Classical Liberalism

| Classical Liberalism | Neo-Liberalism                      |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| 18th–19th century    | 20th century revival                |
| Adam Smith           | Reagan/Thatcher era                 |
| Free market          | Globalized capitalism               |
| Limited government   | Strong privatization + deregulation |

---

# 3. Fundamentals of Neo-Liberalism

# 1. Free Market Economy

Government should NOT interfere in markets.

Prices should be controlled by:

- demand
- supply
- competition

This is linked to:

# Laissez-Faire capitalism

# 2. Opposite of Communism

Neo-liberalism and communism are opposites.

## Communism says:

- state controls economy
- collective ownership
- equality

## Neo-liberalism says:

- private ownership
- free market
- competition
- profit

---

# 3. Everything Becomes a Commodity

Neo-liberalism treats almost everything as something to be bought and sold.

Examples:

- education
- healthcare
- water
- transport
- housing

---

# 4. Deregulation

Government removes restrictions from businesses.

Examples:

- fewer business rules
- fewer labor protections
- less state control

---

# Criticism:

Too much deregulation can:

- increase exploitation
- create monopolies
- cause economic crises

Example:  
2008 financial crisis.

---

# 5. Rule of Market

Society starts thinking through:

- profit
- efficiency
- competition

Everything is judged economically.

---

## Example

Instead of asking:

> “Is this morally right?”

Neo-liberalism asks:

> “Is this profitable?”

---

# 6. Reduction of Public Expenditure

Governments reduce spending on:

- welfare
- subsidies
- public services

Why?  
Neo-liberalism believes:  
Private sector is more efficient.

---

# 7. Privatization

Government institutions become private businesses.

Examples:

- private hospitals
- private schools

---

# Criticism:

Privatization may:

- increase inequality
- make services expensive
- exclude poor people

---

# 9. We Are Consumers, Not Citizens

Neo-liberalism changes identity.

Instead of:

- active citizens  
   people become:
- consumers

Meaning:  
People are valued based on:

- buying power
- market participation

---

# Continuous Survival Mode

People constantly compete for:

- jobs
- income
- survival
- productivity

This creates:

- anxiety
- burnout
- insecurity

---

# 4. Myth of Independence

> Powerful countries manipulate weaker countries for economic and political benefit.

# 5. Rationality (Max Weber)

Weber explained different types of rationality.

---

# A. Instrumental / Market Rationality

Also called:

# Means-End Rationality

You calculate:

- profit
- efficiency
- benefit

Example:  
Choosing the cheapest business option.

---

# Neo-liberalism uses THIS rationality.

Everything becomes:

- calculation
- competition
- efficiency

---

# B. Value-Oriented Rationality

Actions based on:

- ethics
- morality
- principles
- humanity

Even if there is personal loss.

---

## Your Example

Helping accident victim even if you get late.

You sacrificed personal benefit for moral value.

---

# State Welfare and Value Rationality

Your instructor said:

> “Governments work on value-oriented rationality.”

Example:

- metro bus
- welfare
- subsidies
- public hospitals

These may not maximize profit,  
but help society.

---

# C. Crude Rationality

Your note:

> “You didn’t help due to x,y,z reasons.”

This likely refers to:  
self-centered practical thinking.

Example:  
“I may get late.”  
“I don’t want trouble.”

It is close to instrumental self-interest.

---

# 6. Instrumental vs Value-Oriented Rationality

| Instrumental Rationality | Value-Oriented Rationality |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| Profit focused           | Morality focused           |
| Efficiency               | Ethics                     |
| Self-interest            | Social good                |
| Market logic             | Human values               |
| Neo-liberalism           | Welfare state              |

---

# 7. Socialism vs Communism

Students often confuse these.

---

# Socialism

Government controls major sectors to reduce inequality.

But:

- democracy may still exist
- private property may partially exist

Goal:  
Social welfare + equality.

---

# Communism

More radical.

Goal:

- classless society
- no private property
- collective ownership

Karl Marx believed:  
workers should control production.
