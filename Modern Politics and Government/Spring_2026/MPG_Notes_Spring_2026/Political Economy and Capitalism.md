# 1. What is Political Economy?

Political Economy means:

> Study of how politics and economics affect each other.

It studies:

- Who controls wealth?
- Who controls production?
- How governments influence the economy?
- Why some people become rich and others poor?

---

# 2. What is Capitalism?

Capitalism is an economic and political system where:

- Private individuals own businesses
- People invest money to make profit
- Means of production are privately owned

---

## Main Goal of Capitalism

### PROFIT

Example:  
You open a factory.

You:

- own machines
- buy raw material
- hire workers
- sell products

The profit becomes yours.

That is capitalism.

---

# 3. Means of Production

This is VERY IMPORTANT.

Means of production means:

- factories
- machines
- land
- electricity systems
- tools
- raw materials

Whoever owns these controls production.

---

# 4. Mercantilism vs Capitalism

## Mercantilism (16th–17th Century)

Mercantilism was an early economic system where:

> The STATE wanted to become rich.

Countries became rich through:

- colonies
- gold
- trade control
- exploitation of other states

Example:  
Britain taking resources from colonies.

### Key Idea:

State profit was important.

---

## Industrial Capitalism

Later capitalism changed.

Now:

- Individuals own factories
- Individuals make profit

Instead of kings/states becoming rich,  
private businessmen became rich.

Example:  
Factory owners during Industrial Revolution.

---

# 6. Alienation (Karl Marx)

> “You create something but it belongs to capitalist.”

This is Marx’s concept of ALIENATION.

---

## What is Alienation?

Workers produce goods but:

- they do not own products
- they do not control production
- they only receive wages

Example:  
A Nike worker makes shoes.  
But:

- shoes belong to company
- worker gets small salary only

---

## Why Alienation Happens?

Because capitalism separates workers from:

- products
- creativity
- ownership
- control

Workers become “machine-like.”

---

# 7. Laissez-Faire Capitalism

French term meaning:

> “Let people do.”

---

## Idea

Government should NOT interfere in economy.

Markets should work freely.

---

# Adam Smith

Famous thinker.

Known as:

> Father of Capitalism

He believed:

- free markets create efficiency
- competition improves economy
- self-interest benefits society

---

## Invisible Hand

Adam Smith said:

People seeking personal profit indirectly help society.

Example:  
A baker wants profit,  
but society also gets bread.

---

# 8. Recession in Capitalism

Industrial capitalism increased machines.

Machines:

- increase production
- reduce workers

Problem:  
Workers lose jobs.

Then:

- fewer people can buy products
- demand decreases
- factories suffer losses

This creates:

# Recession

---

# 9. John Maynard Keynes

BOOK:

### “The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money”

---

# Keynes’ Main Idea

Capitalism cannot always heal itself.

Government intervention is necessary.

---

## Keynesian Theory

Government should:

- create jobs
- spend money
- support economy during recession

Because free market alone may fail.

---

# 11. Surplus Value (Karl Marx)

Workers produce MORE value than the wages they receive.

The extra value becomes profit for capitalist.

---

## Example

Worker produces:  
$100 worth of products.

Worker receives:  
$30 salary.

Remaining:  
$70 = surplus value (profit).

According to Marx:  
This is exploitation.

---

- reduces inequality

Goal:  
Save capitalism from collapse.
